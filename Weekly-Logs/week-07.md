##  What I Learned

In this week, I learned about **ethics in computing**, which means understanding what is right and wrong while designing and using computer systems.

I understood that computing professionals must protect users, avoid harm, respect privacy, and make fair decisions.

---

##  AI Bias

I learned that **AI bias** happens when an AI system produces unfair results due to biased data or design.

### Causes of AI Bias:
- Biased training data  
- Historical discrimination in data  
- Poor system design  

### Example:
An AI hiring system rejected female candidates because it learned from past biased hiring data.

### Solution:
- Use diverse datasets  
- Regular testing  
- Fairness checks  

---

##  Cybersecurity Ethics

Cybersecurity ethics means protecting systems, data, and user privacy responsibly.

### Key Principles:
- Confidentiality (keep data safe)  
- Integrity (no unauthorized changes)  
- Availability (systems should work properly)  

### Ethical Issue:
Hackers or employees accessing data without permission raises serious ethical concerns.

---

##  Ethical Dilemmas

I learned that ethical dilemmas occur when we must choose between two difficult decisions.

### Example:
- Reporting a security issue vs following manager instructions  
- Protecting user data vs company profit  

---

##  Ethical Decision Making

A simple approach includes:
- Identify the problem  
- Consider stakeholders  
- Follow ethical codes (IEEE/ACM)  
- Analyze consequences  
- Make responsible decision  

---

##  Reflection

This week helped me understand that technology is powerful but must be used responsibly. Ethical decisions are very important in AI and cybersecurity to ensure fairness, safety, and trust.
