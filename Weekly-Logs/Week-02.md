##  What I Learned

In this week, I learned how computing has evolved over time and how it has significantly influenced society, the economy, and professional practices. Early computing began with concepts like Charles Babbage’s Analytical Engine and Ada Lovelace’s work on algorithms. Later developments in the 20th century, including Alan Turing’s contributions and the invention of digital computers, laid the foundation for modern computing.

I also learned about the transformation brought by personal computers, the internet, mobile computing, and cloud technologies, which have made information easily accessible worldwide.

The lecture also highlighted the social impact of computing. It has improved communication through social media and messaging platforms, but it also introduced challenges such as cyberbullying, misinformation, and privacy concerns. Computing has also expanded access to education through online learning platforms, although the digital divide remains a significant issue.

In terms of economic impact, computing has transformed industries like banking, healthcare, and retail. Automation and data analytics have improved efficiency and productivity, while new markets such as e-commerce and software services have emerged.

Another key concept was professional responsibility. Computing professionals must follow ethical guidelines such as those defined by ACM, IEEE, and BCS. They must consider the societal impact of their work, including privacy, fairness, and sustainability.
