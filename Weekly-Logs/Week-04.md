---

##  What I Learned

In this week, I learned the importance of personal branding and how it helps in building a strong professional identity as a Computer Science student. Personal branding is about clearly presenting your skills, strengths, values, and career goals so that others can understand your potential and capabilities.

I understood that having technical skills alone is not enough; it is equally important to showcase them through projects, online profiles, and professional behavior. Platforms like GitHub and LinkedIn play a major role in creating this digital presence.

I also learned about networking, which involves building meaningful professional relationships rather than simply asking for jobs. Networking can open opportunities for internships, collaborations, and career growth. Both physical events (such as seminars and hackathons) and online platforms are important for networking.

Another key concept was the role of GitHub as a technical portfolio. It reflects coding skills, project consistency, and documentation ability. Maintaining a clean and organized GitHub profile is essential for making a good impression.

Additionally, I learned how to build a professional LinkedIn profile, including creating a strong headline, writing an effective “About” section, listing skills, and staying active by sharing and engaging with content.
