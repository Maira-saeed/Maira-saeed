# Week 01 - Orientation

##  Topics Covered

- Graduate Attributes
- Self-Assessment

---

##  What I Learned

In this week, I learned that a Computer Science graduate is not only a programmer but also a problem solver, system thinker, and responsible professional. A CS graduate is expected to identify real-world problems, design logical solutions, and implement them using technology.

I also learned that a software developer is responsible for developing applications, writing code, testing, debugging, and maintaining systems. Moreover, understanding how operating systems, networks, databases, and cloud systems work is essential to think beyond just coding.

Another important concept was professional ethics. A CS graduate must protect user data, avoid plagiarism, and use technology responsibly.

Additionally, I learned about graduate attributes, which include technical knowledge, analytical thinking, communication skills, teamwork, ethical responsibility, and time management. These skills are necessary to become a complete professional.

Lastly, the concept of self-assessment was introduced, which helps in identifying strengths and weaknesses in areas like technical skills, communication, teamwork, and learning ability.
